Camera buying guide

The best camera is the one that fits how you shoot. Start with sensor size: full-frame excels in low light and shallow depth of field, while APS-C is lighter and more affordable. Then weigh autofocus, video specs and lens choice.

  • Travel & everyday: compact APS-C bodies like the Lumen X100.
  • Portraits & weddings: full-frame such as the Lumen Z7.
  • Video & vlogging: flip-screen bodies like the Lumen Pocket.

Lens buying guide

Lenses shape your images more than the body does. A fast prime (like a 50mm f/1.4) gives beautiful background blur; a 24-70mm f/2.8 zoom covers most situations in one lens. Match focal length to subject distance and style.

Accessories checklist

  • Fast, reliable memory cards (and a spare).
  • A tripod for low light, landscapes and video.
  • ND filters for bright scenes and long exposures.
  • A comfortable bag that protects your kit.
